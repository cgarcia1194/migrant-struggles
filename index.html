NYC Migrants Struggle to Meet Basic Needs

Newly arrived migrants in NYC face hurdles in finding housing, work, and schooling for their children

By CARLOS GARCIA

October 12, 2023

Since April 2022, more than 116,000 migrants have come to New York City. At least three essential aspects of their lives - housing, work, and education for their children - all come with significant challenges. In July, the city began issuing 60-day eviction notices to adult migrants in its shelters. New York City Mayor Eric Adams announced in late September that the maximum stay going forward for adult migrants in shelters would be halved, to just 30 days. As for work, migrants have flocked to food delivery, as it is perhaps the only way to make a living. This livelihood for migrants has become increasingly fraught, as the NYPD has cracked down on unregistered mopeds outside of shelters. For their children's schooling, migrants enter a public school system with limited resources to accommodate so many children who do not yet speak English.
